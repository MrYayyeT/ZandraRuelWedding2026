How we met

We didn't meet in a rom-com moment. No violins. No dramatic slowmo glances across a crowd. Just two slightly overcaffeinated humans, mildly skeptical about love, wondering if it had been discontinued sometime after Y2K. By the time we crossed paths, we'd both survived a few plot twists. Life had already thrown us its share of "character development," questionable life choices, and the occasional "Is this adulthood or just prank?" phase. We weren't searching for "the one." We were just hoping for something real. Something that didn't require to explain our jokes or judge our outdated song choices. So we prayed. For clarity. For happiness – in whatever form. Or maybe… for someone who felt like home. And then… we met. No fireworks. Just a oddly calm feeling. Like the universe finally stopped playing games and said, "Okay, fine. Here you go. Don't mess it up." It felt familiar. Like we'd met before. Maybe in another life. Or maybe just in a meme comment section. But this time, destiny was in a good mood and we weren't socially awkward anymore. Our brains couldn't explain it, but our hearts did knew: "This is it. This is the One." We talked. We laughed. We stayed. And slowly, it all started to make sense: Why the delays. Why the almosts. Why we had to go through the emotional Hunger Games first. Turns out, God really does make everything beautiful in His time. Now here we are: Two people who've seen life in all its chaos and still believe in love. Not the kind that sweeps you off your feet, but the kind that shows up, holds your hand, and says: "I'm here. I'm yours. I'm home." "It was a million tiny little things that, when you added them all up, they meant we were supposed to be together." - Sleepless in Seattle (aka the OG slow-burn) And honestly? It was worth every second of the wait. Because this – this love – feels like the universe finally got the memo.
